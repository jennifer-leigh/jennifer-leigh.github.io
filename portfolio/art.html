Art Portfolio

'Untitled', May 2020

Based off a photo taken near my home in Cheshire, this painting is an accumulation of experiments in abstract and Impressionist art. Since moving to the countryside, I have always been grateful to be surrounded by animals – they are regularly a source of inspiration for me.

'Panda Pals', May 2020

In January 2012, I was honoured to be awarded the top prize in the Chinese National Embassy's drawing competition, welcoming the first pandas to the UK. While my felt tip and Pritt stick collage demonstrated little skill beyond the average 10 year old, winning such a competition was a huge landmark in my life.

This digital painting, completed 8 years later, is an ode to how much this competition inspired me and encouraged me to continue creating.

'Tropical Flowers', July 2020

One of my favourite media to work in is Japanese gansai – their rich, saturated colours truly appeal to the colour enthusiast in me!

My inspiration for this painting came from a photo I took in Penang, Malaysia. Whenever I visit family there, I am always struck by the beautiful of the tropics and wildlife I find there, and I wanted to reflect some of this vividness in my painting.

'Covid in the Classroom', April 2020

This optimistic artwork was painted for the Palatinate Newspaper, in response to an article on the potential positives of COVID-19 in inspiring the next generation of scientists, much like the space race for the previous generation.

'King of the Savanna', December 2019

Inspired by Disney's live-action remake of 'The Lion King', this painting was created for the St Chad's (Durham University) Candlemas Ball, titled 'Once Upon a Time in Hollywood', in the style of a movie poster.

    Jenny is an undergraduate student studying Mathematics at Durham University, with a variety of experience in illustration and graphic design.
